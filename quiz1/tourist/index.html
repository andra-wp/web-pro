Uluwatu Temple

A majestic temple perched on a steep 70-meter cliff, offering spectacular sunset views over the Indian Ocean. This site is also famous for its Kecak dance performance held every evening.

Tegallalang Rice Terraces

“Stunning terraced rice fields north of Ubud, showcasing Bali’s traditional irrigation system called ‘subak’. The lush, layered greenery is one of Bali’s most iconic landscapes.

Kelingking Beach, Nusa Penida

Famous for its cliff formation resembling a T-Rex, this beach offers dramatic views of turquoise waters and hidden white sand below. A true paradise for adventurers and photographers.

Mount Batur

An active volcano popular for early morning hikes. Trekkers are rewarded with magical sunrise views from its summit, overlooking Lake Batur and the surrounding mountains.

Tanah Lot Temple

One of Bali’s most sacred sea temples, set on a large rock formation. During high tide, it appears to float in the ocean, and it becomes especially iconic at sunset.

Garuda Wisnu Kencana

A grand cultural park featuring a colossal statue of Lord Vishnu riding Garuda. It is one of the tallest statues in the world and a proud icon of Bali and Indonesia.
