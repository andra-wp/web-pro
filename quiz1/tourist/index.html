Ulun Danu Temple

Pura Ulun Danu Beratan is a beautiful water temple located on the shores of Lake Beratan in Bedugul, Tabanan. Surrounded by misty mountains and calm waters, the temple appears to float on the lake, creating a serene and mystical atmosphere. Dedicated to Dewi Danu, the goddess of lakes and rivers, it is one of Bali’s most iconic and photographed landmarks. Visitors can enjoy scenic views, boating on the lake, and exploring nearby gardens.

Jatiluwih Rice Terraces

Jatiluwih Rice Terraces in Tabanan are UNESCO-listed iconic landscapes known for their vast, verdant rice fields. The area showcases Bali’s traditional subak irrigation system and offers breathtaking panoramic views. Visitors can walk or cycle through the terraces, enjoying the harmony of nature and Balinese agricultural heritage.

Buyan Lake

Buyan Lake is a tranquil mountain lake located in northern Tabanan, Bali. Surrounded by lush forests and cool air, it’s a perfect spot for nature lovers seeking peace and relaxation. Visitors can enjoy trekking, camping, or simply taking in the serene landscape away from the busy tourist areas.

Eka Karya Botanical Garden

Kebun Raya Eka Karya, located in Bedugul, Tabanan, is Bali’s largest botanical garden. Spread across 157 hectares, it features diverse plant collections, scenic walking paths, and beautiful lakes. Visitors can enjoy leisurely strolls, explore tropical flora, and take in panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Tanah Lot Temple

One of Bali’s most sacred sea temples, set on a large rock formation. During high tide, it appears to float in the ocean, and it becomes especially iconic at sunset.

Vihara Mahavira Bedugul Baturiti

Vihara Mahavira in Bedugul, Baturiti, Tabanan is a stunning Buddhist temple known for its vibrant statues and serene atmosphere. Set amidst cool mountain air and scenic surroundings, it offers a peaceful retreat for visitors. The temple’s colorful architecture and large Buddha statues make it a popular spot for photography and spiritual reflection.
